Welcome to Context-Free Grammar Explorer

Please start by chosing one of the grammar input methods in the menu. There is a handful of grammar examples located in the section for text input, meant to demonstrate the particular algorithms. You can even store a grammar in a HTTP link and share it with others.

Input format:

  • Symbols (terminals and nonterminals) are words over the ASCII letters (a-z, A-Z), digits (0-9), some special characters ( ~ ! @ # * ( ) _ + ' ; : ? ), with no whitespaces.
    • Terminals start with a lowercase letter, digit or a special character, nonterminals start with an uppercase letter (A-Z).
    • They can optionally be indexed by appending the forward slash character ( / ) and a non-empty word over the alphabet.
  • Words consist of terminals/nonterminals, each separated by at least one space character; alternatively eps for empty word

You can immediatelly see which normal forms your grammar conforms to (plus whether the grammar is regular). After you choose a normal form in the menu, your grammar will be converted to it, visualizing the process.

  • Use the buttons to navigate through the conversion process. There are buttons to:
    • see the next/previous step
    • proceed to the next/previous task, skipping substeps
    • go to the end/beginning
  • You can either save the final grammar state, any of the intermediate steps, or discard the changes entirely by leaving the section

If your grammar is in strict Chomsky normal form (or you use the tool to convert to it), you can use the CYK algorithm to parse any word over the terminal alphabet. If the word is produced by your grammar, the tool will display:

  • the parse tree (if multiple parse trees exist, you can go through them in random order)
  • the leftmost & rightmost derivation for the displayed parse tree
